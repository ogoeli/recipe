Abacha and Ugba - African Salad

Abacha and ugba also known as African salad is a cassava based dish from the igbo tribe of eastern Nigeria

Ingridients:

  • 4 cups Abacha
  • 1.5 - 2 cups ugba
  • 1 cup Palm oil
  • 1 Teaspoon edible potash (Akaun)
  • 1 whole fresh fish
  • 2-3 medium stockfish/okporoko(optional)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet or 2 tablespoon ground pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • 4-6 garden eggs(optional)
  • 6-8 utazi leaves
  • 2 tablespoon crayfish powder
  • 5 calabash nutmeg seeds(Ehuru)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ogiri
  • Knorr cubes or any boullion cubes

Preparation:

  1. clean and gut your fish, yhen marinate with salt,curry,knorr cube or seasoning.Leave to marinate in your fridge or a cool place for atleast 30 minutes
  2. Then grill or fry.I prefer grilling,this was grilled for 45minutes at 220 degrees...
  3. Soak the abacha in cold water till soft,this should take roughly 10 minutes,leave to drain in a colander...
  4. Rinse the ugba with warm water to rid it of dirt...
  5. Boil the stockfish with salt and seasonig till soft...
  6. Dissolve the potash in 1 cup of water...
  7. Blend the stoch bonnet,1/2 of the onion,crayfish and ground Ehuru till smooth and set aside...
  8. Roast the calabash nutmeg,peel and grind in a mortar or mill.I'm using pre-ground Ehuru but this is what calabash nutmet looks like in case you're wondering...
  9. Pour the palm oil or saucepan,gradually add the potash mixture into the palm oil,mix thoroughly,ypu'll notice the palm oil will begin to thicken and the colour will turn yellowy.Be careful not to add the residue at the bottom of the bowl Now, add the pepper mix into the palm oil...
  10. Now,add the shredded stockfish,Ogiri,then the ugba,stir and combine...
  11. Then add the abacha,stir and combine
  12. And it's ready. You can mix in your chopped utazi leaves at this time if you wish to.I prefer to just garnish with it.Be careful not to add too much utazi though,it's quite bitter and will affect the the taste of your abacha if added in excess

Garnish with onion rings, garden eggs,utazi leaves and the grilled/fried fish...
